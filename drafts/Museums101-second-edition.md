# *Museums 101* — second edition & museums101.com (draft notes)

*Working notes for positioning the second edition and a companion site (**museums101.com**). Aligns with **Convergence Era** (white paper / HTML), **Museum Vitality Index (MVI)** materials, and the consulting narrative in `Museumplanning.MD`. Not published copy.*

---

## One-sentence frame (preface / hero / anywhere)

**The first edition is still true about what a museum *is*; the second edition is about what a museum is *in*—nonprofit form, civil society, public trust—while the landscape for attention, measurement, and technology has changed.**

---

## What has not changed (the spine)

- **Nonprofit** legal and ethical frame  
- **Civil society** — museums as part of democratic public life  
- **Public trust** — stewardship, transparency, mission over noise  
- Boards, accountability, audiences, collections, operations — the **101 contract** with the reader  

None of that is wrong; it is **why** museums matter.

---

## What has changed (the hall)

Not replacing those chapters — **re-situating** them:

- **Attention and platforms** — where the public story is negotiated before the visit  
- **Event- and program-driven** visitation and membership behavior  
- **Measurement** — ratios and vitality, not raw dollars alone (see **MVI**)  
- **AI / agentic tools** — workplace and interpretive reality, not a single product pitch  
- **Spatial and embodied computing** — at the edge of the building and the screen  
- **Honest treatment of experiments** — web3, loyalty stacks, pilots vs. fad (strategic **literacy**, not mandatory hype)  

**Convergence Era** (*Industrial Design, Artificial Intelligence, and the Transformation of Cultural Systems*) supplies the **systems-level** language for that hall. **MVI** supplies **measurement and benchmarking** under the same new conditions. The second edition stays a **primer**; those works are **extensions**, not duplicates.
