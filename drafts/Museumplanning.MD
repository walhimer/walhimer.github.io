## Core thesis (unchanged business, evolved approach)

- **What hasn’t changed:** Museum Planning LLC still does feasibility, strategic and master planning, exhibition design, opening-day thinking, and hands-on methodology — with Mark personally involved. The books, the track record with universities and cities, and “we build museums” remain the spine.
- **What has changed:** **How** museums compete for attention, legitimacy, and audiences. The approach to the work should foreground **ecosystem fluency**: digital-first behavior, event- and programming-driven visitation, social and creator dynamics, and institutional experimentation at scale.

Institutions that treat these as side channels rather than strategic infrastructure will fall further behind — not overnight, but on the curve of relevance.

## Signals in the wider ecosystem (velocity matters)

These move fast; staying credible means **watching and choosing**, not chasing every label.

- **Major metro museums (e.g. Los Angeles / LACMA-scale ecosystem):** Often operate as **R&D for public behavior** — membership, drops, collaborations, nightlife, cultural crossovers. Not every tactic ports to a small city museum, but **clients feel the gap** between “what we built” and “how people decide to show up.”
- **Social media:** For many institutions, it is where the **public story is negotiated** before the visit. Planning buildings without planning **audience and distribution systems** is incomplete.
- **NODE, Data LAND, Zero10 (and adjacent):** Names and platforms in the **data / membership / loyalty / onchain-adjacent** conversation — experiments in **identity, incentives, and community** at speed. The point is not to adopt every stack; it is to understand **where attention and value are being renegotiated** so advisory work stays ahead of “PDF and a ribbon cutting.”
- **Web3 / blockchain:** The field is split — some pilots (ticketing, membership, provenance), some skepticism. A defensible stance: **strategic literacy** and fit-for-purpose pilots, not mandatory hype.
