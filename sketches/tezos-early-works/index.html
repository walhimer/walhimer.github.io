Early Tezos / Objkt work

A chronological record of the first images and series made while learning diffusion tools, minting on Tezos, and moving toward code-driven work. This page is the companion to the studio notes from 2022–2023: progression, not a polished essay.

December 2022

First diffusion piece — after Vera Molnár

The first AI image was made with Stable Diffusion 2.1, prompted with a homage to Vera Molnár (1924–2023, Budapest): “Vera Molnár blue snowflake white background.” It was a way to enter generative image-making through a pioneer of systematic, computer-era art.

2022–2023

Patterns, kinetic sculptures, installation markup

Work continued with abstract patterning in diffusion, then images of three-dimensional kinetic sculptures that existed only as renders, GIFs, and mockups of installation ideas—treating the model as a drafting tool for exhibitions that were never built.

Concept

Cables, connection, shared hours

Parallel ideas: co-creation (code as setup, another hand steering the outcome — after artists such as Leander Herzog on fxhash), and cables as a literal metaphor for connection — USB, serial, power, data — spun into an early p5.js model where color and orientation could be chosen, turning a spool of wire into a participatory object.

Dream Studio / diffusion

Installation mockups — Lozano-Hemmer–inspired scale

Using Dream Studio and related pipelines, the work shifted to large-scale, plausible gallery images: light volumes, grids, and crowds as silhouette. People began asking where they could see the piece; the answer was that it did not exist as a physical install — only as image.

Titles

“Artificial” flowers — images of things that never existed

If the work was already a fiction, the next step was to own it in the title: artificial flowers, plastic flowers — an image of a plastic flower is still artificial. The friction between label and medium became part of the piece.

DALL·E · 2023

Phenomena — light as “matching game”

A separate thread used prompts such as “light caustic effect on a white wall.” The interest was not claiming intelligence for the model but the opposite: large models as statistical matching — the same mechanism applied to natural phenomena (caustics, light behavior) as to text or style.

Surrender machines

Impossible engineering drawings

Surrender machines: imaginary devices that could “surrender” on behalf of the viewer — engineering drawings for mechanisms that cannot be built. As the series ran, color saturation increased; the images moved from blueprint toward painting, then merged with abstract-expressionist energy (Aycock and others as reference points).

March 2023 onward

p5.js, FX, and whole worlds

March 12, 2023 marked the first p5.js abstract piece (#6). Code became the primary medium: canvases informed by Franz Kline, Robert Ryman, Willem de Kooning — not to copy them but to set a stage where variation and series could run. Work on Objkt / Tezos found an audience. The through-line from surrender machines and diffusion became clearer: less a vignette, more a world with a beginning, middle, and end.
